# Help & FAQ

Real questions, asked by real people. If you ask a question twice, the answer
belongs here.

## Why are we rebuilding instead of improving the WordPress sites?

Because the audit showed the problems are **platform fit, not execution**:
page builders impose their patterns, fight custom motion, handle bilingual/RTL
poorly, and ship slow by default (Lighthouse ~32). The brand wants to be
institutional infrastructure; that cannot live inside a page builder. Full
reasoning: [ADR-002](/architecture/adrs).

## Why one codebase for 38 (eventually 110) brands?

At six weeks per separate site, 110 sites ≈ 660 engineer-weeks — thirteen
engineers doing nothing else for a year. Multi-tenant turns each new brand
into configuration. [ADR-001](/architecture/adrs) has the details.

## Why not Shopify for the shop? Why not Contentful/Clerk/etc.?

Subscriptions that scale per-store, per-seat, or per-user **compound** at
110-brand scale, and they hold our data. We self-host what can be self-hosted
and own everything else. See
[what the stack deliberately avoids](/architecture/tech-stack#what-we-deliberately-avoid-and-why).

## Why do we keep Odoo instead of building our own ERP?

Accounting, payroll, and inventory are commodity functions; custom ERPs
consume years and end in regret. We build the *differentiating* layer (portal,
dashboards, AI) on top of Odoo's APIs. [ADR-003](/architecture/adrs).

## Why is the documentation public?

Nothing here is secret — the architecture patterns are industry-standard and
the business positioning is published marketing. Public docs are simpler to
host, shareable with candidates and partners, and force a quality bar.
Credentials and client data never belong here regardless
([Security Basics](/standards/security)).

## I have an urgent request — can I DM the developer?

No — and it's not personal. All work enters through
[one channel](/how-we-work/communication); genuinely urgent items go through
the **operations manager** so the cost (what gets delayed) is decided
deliberately. This is what keeps committed dates honest.

## Who approves my access / reviews my PR / decides priorities?

The CTO (Seif), for all three. See
[Who to Ask](/help/who-to-ask).
