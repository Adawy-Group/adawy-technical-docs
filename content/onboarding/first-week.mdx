# Your First Week

The goal of week one: **understand the why, learn the how, and merge your
first PR.** Asking questions is expected — but check this handbook first, and
when the handbook fails you, fix it.

## Suggested plan

**Day 1 — Setup & context.**
The [Day-1 Checklist](/onboarding/day-one). Accounts, machine, and the three
context reads (About, How We Work, Architecture).

**Day 2 — The platform.**
Read [Tech Stack & Why](/architecture/tech-stack) and the
[ADRs](/architecture/adrs) — the ADRs are the fastest way to absorb how this
team thinks. Then explore the platform monorepo: run an app or two locally
(`apps/group`, `apps/studio`), and walk the `@adawy/*` packages — `ui`,
`design`, `motion`, `i18n`, `seo`, `utils` — to see where the engineering
actually lives.

**Day 3 — The workflow, hands on.**
Read [The Board & Labels](/how-we-work/the-board),
[Git Workflow](/how-we-work/git-workflow), and
[Definition of Done](/how-we-work/definition-of-done). Watch the board for a
day: see how cards move, how PRs link to cards, how the WIP limit behaves.

**Day 4 — Standards + first card.**
Read all of [Standards](/standards). Pick your first card from *This Week*
(the lead will have flagged a good starter — typically a small Content or
Maintenance card, or a fix to this handbook).

**Day 5 — Ship it.**
Take the card through the full cycle: branch → PR linked to the card →
preview → review → merge → verified on staging → Done. That full loop, not the
size of the change, is the point — see [Your First PR](/onboarding/first-pr).

## People to talk to this week

- **The lead** — 30 minutes on the roadmap and where you fit.
- **The designer** — how design hands off to development, where the Figma
  source of truth lives, how design tokens map to code.
